
# Proposal

The Impact of Social Media Use on Mental Health Among Undergraduate Students
David Penn
Department – CECS
________________________________________
Background
Social media has become a central component of daily life for college students, with platforms such as Instagram, TikTok, and X (formerly Twitter) shaping communication, identity, and social interaction. While these platforms provide opportunities for connection and self-expression, growing research suggests a correlation between heavy social media use and negative mental health outcomes, including anxiety, depression, and decreased self-esteem.
Studies have shown that increased screen time and social comparison behaviors can exacerbate psychological distress (Twenge et al., 2018). Additionally, passive consumption of social media content, such as scrolling without interaction, has been associated with lower well-being (Verduyn et al., 2017). However, the relationship is complex, as social media can also offer support networks and access to mental health resources.
Given the prevalence of social media use among college populations, further research is needed to better understand how different usage patterns relate to student mental health within a university setting.
________________________________________
Methodology
This study will use quantitative survey design.
Survey Data Collection
A structured online survey will be distributed to approximately 50 undergraduate students at UCF using Qualtrics.
The survey will measure:
•	Daily social media usage (self-reported hours and platforms used) 
•	Type of engagement (active vs. passive use) 
•	Mental health indicators using validated scales: 
o	PHQ-9 (Patient Health Questionnaire-9) for depression (Kroenke et al., 2001) 
o	GAD-7 (Generalized Anxiety Disorder-7) for anxiety (Spitzer et al., 2006) 
o	Rosenberg Self-Esteem Scale (RSES) for self-esteem (Rosenberg, 1965) 
Open-Ended Questions
To provide qualitative insight without interviews, the survey will include 2–3 open-ended questions, such as:
•	“How does social media affect your mood or mental health?” 
•	“What habits have you developed to manage your social media use?” 
•	“Describe a positive or negative experience you’ve had with social media.” 
Data Analysis
•	Quantitative data will be analyzed using descriptive statistics and correlation analysis 
•	Open-ended responses will be analyzed using basic thematic coding 
Note: This study will identify correlations, not causation, between social media use and mental health outcomes.
________________________________________
Anticipated Outcomes
The study is expected to reveal a correlation between higher levels of social media use—particularly passive consumption—and increased symptoms of anxiety and depression among undergraduate students. It may also identify healthier usage behaviors that are associated with better mental health outcomes.
________________________________________
Significance (Use of the Project)
This research will contribute to a better understanding of how social media relates to student mental health in a university environment. The findings may inform campus wellness initiatives, counseling services, and student awareness programs aimed at promoting healthier digital habits.
