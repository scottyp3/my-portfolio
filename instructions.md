

# Instructions

How to Connect a Wireless Bluetooth Mouse to a Windows Laptop
A wireless Bluetooth mouse allows you to control a Windows laptop without using the trackpad.
________________________________________
Instructions
Step 1.
Slide the power switch so it is aligned with the “ON” position (see Figure 1).
Disclaimer: The location and appearance of the power switch may vary depending on the specific model you are using.

 <img width="1045" height="897" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/cef6f127-ac05-4c3c-8ac2-4b825b441f43" />

Figure 1. Wireless mouse power switch location (Source: https://www.drivereasy.com/knowledge/how-to-connect-wireless-mouse-to-windows-mac/)
________________________________________

Step 2.
On your laptop, click the Start menu located at the bottom of the screen (blue Windows icon, see Figure 2). 
 <img width="1007" height="820" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/c6926898-6cc4-4db2-bf69-e3c2ed6aeab3" />

Figure 2. Opening the Windows Start menu. Screenshot by David Penn.
________________________________________
Step 3.
Click the Settings icon (gear symbol) in the Start menu.
________________________________________





Step 4.
Click Bluetooth & devices in the Settings window (see Figure 3).
 <img width="821" height="539" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/10b5dcd8-36d4-4c75-9072-dbfabd7010b1" />

Figure 3. Accessing Bluetooth settings in Windows. Screenshot by David Penn.
________________________________________
Step 5.
Click Add device.
________________________________________
Step 6.
Select Bluetooth from the list of device types.
________________________________________
Step 7.
Click the name of your wireless mouse when it appears in the list
(it may appear as the brand name, e.g., Logitech M30).
________________________________________
Step 8.
Move the mouse to confirm that the cursor moves on the screen.
